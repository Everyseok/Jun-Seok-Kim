About Our Laboratory

The Lab of Combustion and Technology Convergence (LCTC) investigates both fundamental and applied combustion phenomena with a focus on energy efficiency, safety, and environmental sustainability. Our work integrates combustion chemistry, fluid mechanics, heat transfer, and electrohydrodynamics to reveal governing mechanisms and deliver practical solutions.

Core topics include flame dynamics under externally applied electric fields (DC/AC), ionic-wind–assisted smoke control and fire suppression, soot formation and radiative heat transfer, and diagnostics using high-speed imaging and optical methods. We complement experiments with reduced-order modeling and high-fidelity simulations to develop predictive frameworks.

Through cross-disciplinary collaboration, we aim to bridge classical combustion science with emerging technologies in propulsion, power generation, and fire safety. The laboratory provides rigorous training for students while delivering research outcomes that support a cleaner and safer energy future.

Ultimately, LCTC seeks to advance fundamental understanding while translating insights into scalable technologies—contributing to the next generation of combustion-enabled and combustion-assisted energy systems.
